Refund Policy

Clear refund rules for ticket buyers and event organizers.

This policy explains how Event Sphere handles refund requests for canceled, postponed, rescheduled, and organizer-managed events. Specific event terms may apply when they are shown before checkout.

1. General policy

Tickets are typically final sale unless the event is canceled, a refund is required by law, or the organizer has published a refund option for that event. Event Sphere reviews refund requests based on the event status, organizer policy, order record, and timing of the request.

2. Canceled events

If an organizer cancels an event and does not provide a replacement date, eligible ticket holders may receive a refund of the ticket price according to the organizer's cancellation instructions and applicable law. Event Sphere may need confirmation from the organizer before processing the refund.

3. Postponed or rescheduled events

When an event is postponed or rescheduled, tickets usually remain valid for the new date. If the organizer opens a refund window, ticket holders must submit requests within the stated deadline. Requests submitted after the window closes may be declined unless required by law.

4. Organizer refund terms

Some organizers offer flexible refund terms, transfer options, or limited refund windows. When these terms are shown on the event page or during checkout, they become part of the purchase terms for that event.

5. Fees and non-refundable amounts

Service, processing, payment, or delivery fees may be non-refundable unless required by law or expressly included in the refund. When a refund is approved, Event Sphere will show the eligible amount before the refund is finalized where possible.

6. How to request a refund

  • Open the Help Center or email support@eventsphere.com.
  • Include the event name, order email, ticket quantity, and reason for the request.
  • Attach any organizer communication or screenshots that support the request.
  • Submit the request before any announced refund deadline.

7. Refund timing

Approved refunds are usually submitted to the original payment method. Bank and payment processor timelines vary, and it may take several business days for the funds to appear after Event Sphere or the organizer initiates the refund.

8. Chargebacks and duplicate requests

If you open a payment dispute with your bank, Event Sphere may be unable to continue a standard refund review until the dispute is resolved. Submitting duplicate requests can slow review times, so please keep communication in one support thread whenever possible.

9. Event entry issues

If you are denied entry at an event, contact event staff immediately and keep proof of the issue. Refund eligibility depends on the reason for denial, event rules, ticket validity, and organizer verification.
